During my time at the National Institute of Public Finance and Policy (NIPFP), I worked on research examining air pollution trends in India using statistical techniques. This project involved collecting and processing large datasets of pollution measurements and applying econometric methods to identify patterns and causal factors.

The work resulted in a series of blog articles and a research paper that analyzed the determinants of air pollution levels across Indian cities, examining the role of industrial activity, vehicular emissions, and seasonal variation. The analysis used time-series methods and panel data techniques to draw inferences about pollution dynamics and policy interventions.

This research contributed to the broader conversation on environmental policy in India, providing data-driven evidence on the effectiveness of regulatory measures aimed at reducing particulate matter and other pollutants.
