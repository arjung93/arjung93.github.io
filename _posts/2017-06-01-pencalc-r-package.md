At the National Institute of Public Finance and Policy (NIPFP), I developed **PenCalc**, an R package designed to simulate the distribution of pension payouts at retirement age. The package was accompanied by a quantitative research paper analyzing pension adequacy under different contribution and return scenarios.

PenCalc models the stochastic nature of retirement savings by simulating pension accumulation paths under varying assumptions about contribution rates, investment returns, and retirement ages. The tool is useful for policymakers and researchers studying the fiscal implications of pension systems, particularly in the context of defined-contribution schemes.

The package allows users to:
- Simulate pension distributions under customizable parameters
- Visualize the range of possible retirement outcomes
- Assess the probability of pension adequacy given different policy scenarios

This work was developed in the context of ongoing pension reform discussions in India, and the package was used to inform presentations and policy analysis at NIPFP.
